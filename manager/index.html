Yusuke Tsutsumi's management philosophy

This page explains my philosophy as a manager.

I’m here to help you be the best you can be (at least professionally)

First and foremost, I’m here as your partner in your career and professionally. That means that I’ll advocate for you in performance reviews, try to give you honest feedback on how you’re doing, and will ask you hard questions if it’s in the interest of helping you grow.

No person is perfect, but my intent is to drive you toward success in your desired career path, whatever that may be.

How I grow people

In my experience, people who really succeed in their craft:

  • know how to find high-impact projects that help their peers and organization
  • have the technical ability to deliver said projects

So I focus on building those skills first and foremost.

Teaching you to grow your own career

Although your level and title isn’t a complete reflection of your abilities, it’s an important one to focus on. Title and level can impact future career opportunities, compensation, and sometimes the clout you bring to a conversation.

In this process, I believe it is my job to help you better understand the expectations at each level, and help you better evaluate whether the work you’ve been doing is a good representation or not. If you are able to evaluate yourself accurately, you have a much tigher feedback loop when considering which opportunities are worth undertaking from a career perspective.

How I Lead

Growing on the process, not the result

If you already work with me, then you might have noticed I don’t like to hand out my perspective of answer right away.

That’s because I’m more focused on your ability to come to a near-optimal answer, rather than getting the optimal answer quicky. In other words, I want your input coming up with the solution, rather than expecting you to implement my proposed solution. So I’d rather sit with you, ask your perspective, and ask clarifying questions and share perspective that helps inform your opinion. Again, my goal is to provide you with lifelong tools, than one-off dictations.

I’m also concerned with the optimal answer more than I am about being right. I try to have strong data to back up my arguments, but please share your perspective and data so we can come to a consensus together on the right solution.
