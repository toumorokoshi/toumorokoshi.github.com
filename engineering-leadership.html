Thoughts on Engineering Leadership

Thoughts on the attributes and skills of a good engineering leader: one who is guiding others on their projects, rather than being directly involved.

Checklist

  • Try to connect the SWEs working on something directly with the customer who’s using them: eliminate the middleperson from the discussion.
  • Try to give SWEs a problem, not the solution: giving a solution doesn’t motivate the SWE to take ownership. Instead, try to crystalize the problem enough that the SWE can take that ownership and know what to do, or at least how to get started.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask why and push on design decisions: decisions are made for a variety of reasons outside of being the globally optimal one, including time constraints, or other tech leads dictating a position. It’s okay to ask why and explore for a more optimal way of doing things.
  • Delegate, delegate, delegate: if someone else wants to own a project, let them! there’s new opportunities everywhere, and you can get help guide the solution.
  • Try to amplify good ideas bottoms-up: listen to your engineers, help them shape their proposal. When it’s good enough, help advocate for it at higher forums.
