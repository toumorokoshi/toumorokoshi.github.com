Best Practices for Life

This page lists some best practices I’ve learned to live a better life. Someday perhaps I’ll make this a book.

Teeth

  • Brush and floss your teeth at least twice a day.
  • In the morning, brush your teeth before you eat to clean off the bacteria that has multiplied overnight in your mouth.
  • Try to have longer chunks of no eating to give your teeth a chance to remineralize.

Exercise

  • Participate in exercises that improve bone health.
    • Brazilian Jujitsu (the study says MMA fighters in general have better bone density, but the brain trauma isn’t worth it).
    • Weight lifting

Exercises that improve bone health

You should participate in activities that improve bone health, as early in life as possible.

Bone density tends to go down with age.

Sleep

  • Get 7-8 hours of sleep a night.

Diet

  • Eat near zero saturated fat
  • Eat 1.6g / kg body weight of protein daily
  • Eat less than 25g added sugar daily
  • Eat 30g+ fiber a day

Eat near zero saturated fat

Saturated fat intake for many causes and increase in ldlc / apob which can result in clogged arteries an ASCVD..

As an excerpt:

If you pharmacologically lower ApoB to somewhere in the 20 to 30 milligram per deciliter range for everybody in the population while someone is in their 20s, can you eliminate ASCVD? ⇒ I think the answer is probably yes

Eat a high fiber diet (30+g)

Anecdotally, a high fiber diet results in a lowered ldlc.

Office Work

  • Walk on a treadmill desk.
  • Use text-to-speech instead of typing to help reduce your typing and risk of repetitive stress injury.

Food

  • Eat a plant-based diet (vegan). Vegans live 7 years longer on average.
  • Don’t do intermittent fasting without exercise. Paired with a sedentary lifestyle, it’ll increase your body fat significantly.

Mind Hacking

  • Use Anki / spaced repetition to memorize anything.
