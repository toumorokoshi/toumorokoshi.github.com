Value-focused mindset

I think developers always need to start a project with a goal:

  • what precisely do you want to achieve?
  • How do you measure success?
  • Do you have a specific user who will use this as soon as it’s out?

Work backwards from there.

Many developers are adept at solving technical problems, but solutions are often thrown away not because of a technical reason, but because the solution doesn’t really match something that was really needed.

Sticking Landings

In the same vein of looking at the value being delivered: how do you stick landings? Often, this comes with some work beyond the technical side:

  • promotion
    • how will you evangelize this?
    • how will people learn about this new feature?

A learning loop

How do you learn outside of your regular responsibilities? Do you read technical news websites? blog posts? white papers? How do you discover them?

Albert Einstein was able to come up with the General Theory of Relativity because he was aware of non-euclidian geometry. In other words, you can’t harness technology or knowledge if you don’t know that it exists in the first place.

The problem with only learning by doing the tasks you’re assigned, is that you’ll miss ideas that are novel, maybe to even your team or your organization.

The best developers bring ideas that the team has never heard of. To do that, you need to discover ideas that you’ve never heard of.
